The federal gap, measured: how much of each state's exclusion list never reaches the LEIE

state exclusions not on the federal list · updated daily

Federal regulation requires screening against the federal databases — the OIG LEIE and SAM.gov — every month (42 CFR 455.436). But states exclude providers for reasons that never propagate to the federal lists: license actions, state program-integrity findings, Medicaid fraud-unit referrals, terminations for cause. Measured across every state list we ingest, about half of active state exclusions do not appear on the federal LEIE — and in some states the share exceeds 90%. Under ACA §6501, an exclusion or for-cause termination in one state's Medicaid program is grounds for termination in every other state, so each row below is exposure a federal-only screen never sees.

The number that matters: a compliance program screening only the LEIE misses ~53% of active state-level exclusion records — tens of thousands of provider exclusions invisible to federal-only screening. Every figure on this page recomputes from the live dataset each day.

How we measure it

Plain-English methodology, no tricks:

  • What's being compared. Every currently-active exclusion record from each state's own published list, against the current federal OIG LEIE — both sides from the same daily-refreshed dataset, loaded from the agencies' own publications.
  • How a match is decided. A state record counts as "on the LEIE" when it resolves to the same provider on the federal list — NPI-verified matches first (validated against the CMS NPPES registry), then exact-identity matching. Ambiguous records are counted conservatively.
  • What the gap is. The percentage of a state's active exclusion records with no corresponding federal LEIE record. It is a measure of visibility, not of wrongdoing: states exclude for state-law reasons the federal process may never adopt.
  • What it is not. Not a criticism of any agency — states publish exactly what their mandate requires. The gap exists because the two systems have different scopes; compliance teams simply need to see both.
  • Freshness. Sources are re-fetched and the gap recomputed daily; the as-of timestamp above comes from the dataset itself.
