WHY IT MATTERS

Screening only the federal list misses half the risk

States exclude providers for reasons that never propagate to the federal LEIE — and when they do propagate, it happens with months of lag. The exposure is concrete: payments involving excluded parties risk overpayment recoupment and Civil Monetary Penalties, and under ACA §6501 an exclusion in one state's Medicaid program is grounds for termination in every other state. RosterProof closes that gap for providers and vendors — billing companies, DME suppliers, staffing agencies appear on these lists as entities — with every record carrying its source, jurisdiction, exclusion window, and an NPI match with stated confidence, so a compliance analyst can defend every hit without asking anyone.

How matching works: four tiers, strongest first

Every match carries its tier, evidence, and confidence. Unresolved records stay honestly unresolved.

T1
Source-published NPI, NPPES-verifiedthe excluding agency printed an NPI and the CMS NPPES registry confirms the identity
T2
Unique identity match (name+state)exactly one provider in the national registry shares the normalized name and state — including registered maiden/former/dba aliases
T3
License-number corroboratedthe state license number on the exclusion agrees with the registry
T4
AI-adjudicated, human review requireda large language model weighs the remaining evidence, writes its reasoning, and every verdict is flagged for human review
